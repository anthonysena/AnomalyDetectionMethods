Purpose

This dataset mixes:

  • two dense clusters that represent valid but multimodal structure
  • one sparse local anomaly near the first cluster
  • one strong global anomaly far from both clusters
  • one heavy-tailed feature pattern through featureC

That mix is useful for showing the practical tradeoffs:

  • LOF is strong at local density departures
  • Isolation Forest is strong at broad isolation-based ranking
  • Gaussian anomaly detection is easy to interpret geometrically, but is more sensitive to multimodality and shape assumptions

Reading The Results

  • Rows with high LOF but moderate Gaussian distance are often local density anomalies rather than global geometric outliers.
  • Rows with high Isolation Forest score across several seeds are stable tree-based anomalies.
  • Rows that remain extreme under both classical and robust GAD are strong candidates for structural outliers rather than covariance artifacts.
  • Disagreement across methods is informative: it usually tells you whether the anomaly is local, global, or driven by shape assumptions.
